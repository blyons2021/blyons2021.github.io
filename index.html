Blog Post 1: Thoughts on "The Pragmatic Programmer"

In Chapter 1 of "The Pragmatic Programmer", the section that stood out to me the most was section 2, "Software Entropy". As described in the beginning of this section, "Entropy is a term used in physics that refers to the amount of disorder in a system." Being a physics major and knowing what entropy is, I thought this was an interesting way to describe the idea of disorder. The increase of disorder in software is referred to as "software rot", which I thought was another interesting way to describe disorder in general when working on a group project. Being a junior, I have ABSOLUTELY experienced software rot (especially in Data Structures & Algorithms, as well as Networking).

Later on in this section, something called the "Broken Window Theory" was introduced. This is described as something implemented in "...police departments in New York and other major cities to crack down on the small stuff in order to keep out the big stuff." Connecting this to software jargan, the broken windows represent bad designs, wrong decisions, and poor code. This idea of the "Broken Window Theory" is interesting to me; it is easy to connect this idea to precautions in things like choosing the theme of the project, what data structures to use, how to implement various things to make the project work, etc. The imagery in these examples allow me to better visualize the metaphor and learn how it is relavent to the process of working on a group software project.
